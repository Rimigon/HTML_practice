Auguste Comte

The founder of sociology as an independent science

Isidore Marie Auguste François Xavier Comte

Here's a time line of Auguste Comte life:

  • 1798 - born in Montpellier, Hérault on 19 January, at the time under the rule of the newly founded French First Republic
  • 1817 - found an apartment at 36 Rue Bonaparte in Paris's 6th arrondissement (where he lived until 1822) and later that year he became a student and secretary to Henri de Saint-Simon, who brought Comte into contact with intellectual society and greatly influenced his thought therefrom
  • 1822 - published a Plan de travaux scientifiques nécessaires pour réorganiser la société (Plan of scientific studies necessary for the reorganization of society)
  • 1824 - left Saint-Simon, again because of unbridgeable differences
  • 1825 - married Caroline Massin
  • 1826 - he was taken to a mental health hospital, but left without being cured – only stabilized by French alienist Jean-Étienne Dominique Esquirol – so that he could work again on his plan (he would later attempt suicide in 1827 by jumping off the Pont des Arts)
  • 1842 - divorce with Caroline Massin
  • 1844 - he fell deeply in love with the Catholic Clotilde de Vaux, although because she was not divorced from her first husband, their love was never consummated. After her death in 1846 this love became quasi-religious, and Comte, working closely with Mill (who was refining his own such system) developed a new "Religion of Humanity"
  • 1851–1854 - published four volumes of Système de politique positive
  • 1856 - the first volume of La Synthèse Subjective ("The Subjective Synthesis")
  • 1857 - died in Paris on 5 September
